What is AI?


Artificial Intelligence — or AI — refers to computer systems that can carry out tasks we would normally expect to need human thinking: understanding language, recognising patterns, summarising information, and generating text.


One of the most useful types of AI right now is called a Large Language Model, or LLM. You may have heard of tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or Copilot — these are all LLMs.


Throughout this website, we will use the word AI to refer to these kinds of tools. You do not need to know how they work under the hood to use them well — but understanding a few basics will help you get better results and avoid common mistakes.

Can AI make mistakes?


Yes — and this is one of the most important things to understand before you start.


AI tools can produce incorrect information, miss important nuances, or reflect biases from the data they were trained on. They can sound very confident even when they are wrong. This is sometimes called 'hallucination' — the AI generates something that sounds plausible but is not accurate.


The example shows how AI can oversimplify a nuanced community response:

“The new park is beautiful, but wheelchair access is still very limited.”
